Smart Stick for the Blind

An IoT-based assistive device for enhanced navigation and safety.

The Project Story

Traditional canes offer limited environmental awareness, posing safety risks from obstacles above ground or sudden terrain changes like water puddles. To address this, I designed and built a "smart stick" that integrates multiple sensors to create a comprehensive safety device.

Using an Arduino microcontroller, the stick is equipped with ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection and a water sensor at the tip. It provides real-time alerts to the user through a combination of haptic feedback (vibrations) and audio buzzers, dramatically improving situational awareness and user independence. Calibrating the sensors and ensuring the device was both lightweight and power-efficient were the primary engineering challenges. This project solidified my skills in hardware integration and user-centric design.

Technology Stack

  • Arduino (C/C++)
  • Ultrasonic Sensors
  • Water Sensor
  • Vibration Motor & Buzzer

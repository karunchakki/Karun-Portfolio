Smart Stick for the Blind

An IoT-based assistive device for enhanced navigation and safety.

The Problem

Traditional canes offer limited environmental awareness for visually impaired individuals, particularly for obstacles above ground level or sudden changes in terrain like water puddles, posing significant safety risks.

My Solution

I designed and built a "smart stick" that integrates multiple sensors to create a comprehensive safety device. Using an Arduino microcontroller, the stick is equipped with ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection and a water sensor at the tip. It provides real-time alerts to the user through a combination of haptic feedback (vibrations) and audio buzzers, dramatically improving situational awareness and user independence.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

Calibrating the range of the ultrasonic sensors and ensuring the device was both lightweight and power-efficient were the primary engineering challenges. This project was a valuable exercise in user-centric design, forcing me to think critically about how technology can be practically applied to solve real-life human problems. It solidified my skills in hardware integration and embedded C programming.

Technology Stack

  • Arduino (C/C++)
  • Ultrasonic Sensors
  • Water Sensor
  • Vibration Motor & Buzzer

Key Features

  • Multi-sensor obstacle detection.
  • Water and puddle detection.
  • Dual-mode haptic and audio alerts.
  • Ergonomic and low-power design.
